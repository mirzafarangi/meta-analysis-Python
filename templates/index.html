Meta-Analysis

Meta-analysis is a quantitative, formal, epidemiological study design used to systematically assess previous research studies to derive conclusions about that body of research.

By pooling study-specific estimates of a common effect size and adjusting those estimates for sampling and measurement error, a meta-analyst can generate a weighted mean estimate of the effect size that normally reflects the true population effect size more accurately than any of the individual estimates on which it is based.

Why Meta-Mar?

Meta-Mar is a free online meta-analysis service which is developed for department of psychology in university of Marburg, makes it possible to enter data directly or easily upload an already excisting Microsoft Excel spreadsheet in order to receive the results of the analysis.

Facilities of this primary phase of the project include:

  • Calculation of the effect sizes based on Cohen'd and Hedges' g method for each single study.
  • Calculate the overall effect size of the analysis.
  • Drawing the Forest Plot of the analysis.

Calculations are based on sample sizes, means and standard deviations of each study.

In the second phase of the project, we will develope other calculations of the effect sizes such as ratio odds, correlations and regressions.
